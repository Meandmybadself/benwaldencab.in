Trails Nearby

Hikes along the Gunflint Trail

The Gunflint Trail and surrounding Superior National Forest hold some of Minnesota's finest hiking — from short scenic overlooks like Honeymoon Bluff and Magnetic Rock to Eagle Mountain, the state's highest point, and the rugged Border Route and Kekekabic wilderness trails. These trailheads are all within reach of the cabin.

1

Magnetic Rock Trail

Moderate3.2 mi↑ 420 ft

Out-and-back past glacial erratics to a 25-foot magnetic monolith that deflects a compass. Open, rocky, and burned-over in stretches with big sky views.

2

Honeymoon Bluff Trail

Easy0.4 mi↑ 141 ft

Short, steep stair-climb to a bluff overlooking Hungry Jack and West Bearskin Lakes. A classic sunset spot near the Clearwater Road.

3

Caribou Rock Trail

Moderate3.9 mi↑ 1,161 ft

Ridge-top overlooks of Bearskin, Duncan, and Moss Lakes. Turn around at the first vista or push on toward Stairway Portage.

4

South Lake Trail

Strenuous3.9 mi↑ 1,332 ft

Steep access spur that climbs to the Border Route Trail and the cliffs above South Lake on the Canadian border. Short but a real workout.

5

Centennial Trail

Easy2.7 mi↑ 394 ft

Interpretive loop through the 1999 blowdown and regrowth near the Gunflint Ranger Station, with boardwalks over wetland.

6

Border Route Trail

Strenuous65.2 mi↑ 2,060 ft

Rugged 65-mile ridgeline route along the Canadian border, linking cliff-top lake overlooks across the highlands. Best sampled in day sections.

7

Kekekabic Trail

Strenuous41.0 mi↑ 1,391 ft

Remote 40-mile wilderness traverse from the Gunflint Trail west toward Ely, deep into the Boundary Waters backcountry.

8

Eagle Mountain Trail

Moderate3.2 mi↑ 650 ft

The path to Minnesota's highest point (2,301 ft), passing Whale Lake through boreal forest and rock. Reached by forest road (The Grade) off the Gunflint Trail.

9

Pincushion Mountain Loop

Moderate3.1 mi↑ 505 ft

Network of loops on the Sawtooth ridge above Grand Marais, with a short spur to a granite dome overlooking Lake Superior.
